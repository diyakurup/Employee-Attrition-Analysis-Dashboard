# Employee-Attrition-Analysis-Dashboard
Employee Attrition analysis using Excel and Power BI
This project identifies the primary drivers of organizational turnover and provides data-driven strategies to reduce the current attrition rate from 16.1% to the target of 15%.

### Key Insights & Findings
The Overtime Crisis: Employees working overtime face a 30.5% attrition rate, compared to just 10.4% for those who do not.

High-Risk Tenure: New hires (under 3 years) are the most vulnerable group, with a 30% attrition rate.

Role Vulnerability: The Sales Representative role is at extreme risk with a 39.8% attrition rate, while leadership roles like Research Directors remain stable.

Commute & Demographics: There is a clear correlation between long commutes and higher exit rates; additionally, single employees are more likely to leave (25%) than married or divorced peers.

### Strategic Recommendations
Overtime Mitigation: Audit high-overtime departments and implement a "cap" on consecutive overtime weeks.

New Hire "Buddy" Program: Link "Veteran" employees (10+ years) with new hires to improve cultural integration and engagement.

Remote Work: Offer hybrid options for roles located more than 20 miles from the office to reduce travel fatigue.

Sales Restructuring: Investigate stressors in the Sales department and review commission structures to provide earlier "win" opportunities.

##  Conclusion
By addressing work-life balance and the specific stressors of high-attrition roles, this project provides the framework necessary to stabilize the workforce and protect long-term talent investments.
